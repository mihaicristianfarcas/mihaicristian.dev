Most note apps are filing cabinets. They're good at putting something away and
not much help while an idea is still shapeless and you're pushing it around
trying to see what it is. That second half is the one I wanted.

So Adso is block-based and nests as deep as you like. A thought that turns out
to be three thoughts becomes three thoughts by dragging. A note that grows a
whole subject underneath it can keep it there, and the structure follows the
thinking instead of a folder.

It's named after the novice in *The Name of the Rose*, who spends the entire
book taking notes on something he doesn't understand yet. I related.
