The encryption protecting VPN traffic today is safe against every computer that
currently exists. That's a weaker promise than it sounds: an attacker can record
your traffic now and decrypt it in ten years, once the machines catch up. If
what you're sending has to stay secret for a decade, the deadline has already
passed.

This is my BSc thesis. It runs ML-KEM-768 alongside WireGuard's existing X25519
handshake, on top of Cloudflare's BoringTun. *Hybrid* is the important word here:
ML-KEM is young, and young cryptography sometimes breaks, so I kept the old
handshake underneath it. An attacker has to defeat both, which means adopting it
early can't cost you the security you already had.

As far as I can tell it's the first FIPS 203-compliant hybrid WireGuard on a
production Rust codebase. I benchmarked it end to end on an Apple M4 and a
Raspberry Pi 5, because whether post-quantum cryptography works is settled.
What I wanted to know is what it costs on hardware people actually own.
