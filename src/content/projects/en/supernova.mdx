Supernova renders looping video, GLSL shaders transpiled to Metal, and
sandboxed web bundles behind your desktop icons. Wallpapers can react to audio
and motion, every display gets its own, and playlists rotate them on a
schedule.

Most of the work has gone into knowing when *not* to draw. A wallpaper is the
one piece of software on your machine that has no right to cost you anything,
and the moment it spins up a fan or eats a percent of battery you'll uninstall
it. I would. So it sleeps under covering windows, throttles on battery, and
hands the GPU back the instant nobody's looking.
