Almost all web traffic is encrypted now, and more and more of it is QUIC. Good
for privacy, awkward for anyone who needs to understand their own network: if
you can't read the packets, how do you know what's on the wire?

Traffic has a shape, though. You can't see what a video call is saying, but a
video call *looks* nothing like a file download: different packet sizes,
different rhythm, different bursts. That's enough to tell them apart without
decrypting a single byte.

This captures QUIC packets inside the kernel with eBPF/XDP, extracts those
statistical features, and classifies them into 102 application categories with a
small neural network. I kept it deliberately tiny, under 5 MB and under 10 ms
per inference, so it can sit on the edge device itself instead of shipping
everyone's traffic somewhere else first.
