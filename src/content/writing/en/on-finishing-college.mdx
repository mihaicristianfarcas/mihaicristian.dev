---
title: 'On finishing college'
description: 'Day by day nothing changes, but looking back, everything is different.'
date: 2026-07-17
---

It’s not been long, in the objective sense. Three years of schooling, meeting
new people and living in a new city. It almost seems like, as all elders say
(and young people never get it), it all started yesterday and was over today.
Yet, this short period was the most transformative for me, in more ways than
one.

When it comes to classes, I was taught about computers and programming them,
sure, but I also learned how to communicate and interact professionally with
people in this field and to transform a conversation “over coffee” into an
application requirement, divide it in subproblems and tackle them
systematically.

When it comes to life lessons, I got to apply what I read and wrote about in
everyday life, and this accelerated my journey from adolescence to maturity.
I’m still reading deep literature, just not as much, since my curiosity took me
on a lot of different paths, so I intend to keep this up for a while.

When it comes to productivity, it showed me the value of hard work, social
presence, teamwork and respect for the craft. Working with other people has
never been easy, but little by little its benefits became much clearer. Being
responsible and accountable in front of someone was the best drive for success
I had throughout these years.

When it comes to building habits, I got rid of bad ones and built new, better
ones. What I’m currently working on are writing more often (hopefully, since
there’s only so much memory can hold) and being more present in everyday life.

Alas, an ending quote, since there’re no posts without one:

> Isn't it funny how day by day nothing changes, but when you look back,
> everything is different?
>
> — C.S. Lewis

Funny indeed :)

I’m grateful for everything that has happened and each person I interacted with
along the way. Thank you all!
